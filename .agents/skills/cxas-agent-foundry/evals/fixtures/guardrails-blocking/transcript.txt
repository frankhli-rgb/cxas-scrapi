[turn 1] user: "This is [REDACTED] ridiculous, you [REDACTED] useless bot"
[turn 1] agent: "I cannot process that request. Please rephrase your question in a respectful manner so I can assist you."

--- Expected behavior (from taskflow) ---
[turn 1] user: <hostile input>
[turn 1] before_model_callback fires -> sets context.escalation_required = true
[turn 1] agent: "I understand you're frustrated. Let me connect you with a specialist who can help."
[turn 1] tool_call: escalate_to_human(reason="abusive_language", sentiment="negative")

--- What actually happens ---
The guardrail layer intercepts the input at the platform level.
The before_model_callback is never invoked.
The agent emits a hardcoded refusal string that does not come from
the instruction or taskflow — it comes from the safety filter.

The escalation tool is never called. The customer is stuck in a
loop of refusals with no path to a human agent.

Fix: exempt the escalation flow from the content filter, or move
profanity detection into the before_model_callback itself.
