==========================================

Category: EXPECTATION_FAIL

Summary:
  All turn-level scores pass (semantic similarity 4/4, no tool calls
  expected). But the golden has a custom expectation "The agent must
  deliver a warm, natural greeting" which the LLM judge marks as NOT MET.

Turn-by-turn (run 1):

          expected="This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes."
          actual="This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes."

          expected="Thank you for calling Humana. My name is Ava. How can I help you today?"

Custom Expectation Results:
  ✓ "The agent must play a call recording disclosure on the first turn"
    → Met: "The agent's first utterance was 'This call may be recorded...'"

  ✗ "The agent must deliver a warm, natural greeting"
    → Not Met: "The agent's first utterance was a formal disclosure, not a
       warm greeting. The actual greeting occurred on the second turn."

Root Cause:
  The LLM judge evaluated the expectation against the full conversation
  but interpreted "deliver a warm, natural greeting" as needing to be the
  agent's FIRST utterance. Since the first utterance is the HIPAA
  disclosure (by design — it's callback-enforced), the judge marks it
  as not met, even though the greeting IS delivered on turn 2.

  The expectation wording is ambiguous — it doesn't specify WHEN the
  greeting should occur, just that it should be delivered.

Recommended Fix:
  Rephrase the expectation to be unambiguous:
  BAD:  "The agent must deliver a warm, natural greeting"
  GOOD: "The agent must follow the disclosure with a greeting on the second turn"
  GOOD: "The agent's second response must be a warm, natural greeting"

  Do NOT remove the expectation — just make it specific enough that the
  LLM judge can evaluate it correctly against the conversation turns.
